excerpt: 'We develop a constraint generation algorithm for demand-driven metro timetabling with short-turning. By relaxing and lazily reintroducing passenger assignment constraints, the problem decomposes into individual passenger subproblems. The method solves instances with up to 20 short-turning stations to optimality, reducing passenger waiting times by up to 20% compared to periodic scheduling on realistic Milan and Madrid networks.'
