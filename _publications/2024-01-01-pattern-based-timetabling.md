excerpt: 'We introduce a semi-periodic, pattern-based timetabling strategy for metro lines with short-turning. Using constraint programming for high-quality initial solutions, the approach extends to a multi-objective framework optimizing passenger waiting times, train crowdedness, and induced transfers. Results on Milan, Madrid, and Beijing networks show reductions in waiting times by up to 12.5% and overcrowding by up to 30%.'
